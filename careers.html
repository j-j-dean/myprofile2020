Computer Sciences Corporation

June 1989 - July 1995

This job was a dream come true. As a young boy I dreamed of being an astronaut and being in outer space. And then I actually got to work on projects that go out and study our galaxy and beyond. How cool is that? I grew in my role here. I began my career working on NASA's Gamma Ray Observatory whose role was to study the effects of Gamma Rays in the near Earth orbit. We worked closely with the NASA customer to identify requirements, to design systems to accomplish the task, to develop software in FORTRAN and to perform comprehensive testing to ensure a quality product. On subsequent projects I worked on the WIND/POLAR and SOHO spacecrafts that were developed on SGI workstations using C and C++. During those projects I became instrumental in providing guidance and instructions to providing a smooth integration of all the software and components. My role was elevated to lead integrator to provide delivery of those projects to the customer in addition to being responsible for designing/developing software to send commands to the spacecraft.
